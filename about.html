SUSAN NICOLE ARTS

About Susan Nicole Delgado

Susan Nicole Delgado, also known as Susan Nicole Arts, is a Cuban-American fine artist from Brooklyn, New York. Grounded in the philosophy that "Dreams are the Foundation of Reality," my work focuses on the intersection of classical technique and imaginative storytelling. I am currently in a dedicated discovery phase, focusing on technical mastery and the refinement of my visual language.

Fine Art & Studio

The Fine Art Process

My professional workflow is a rigorous multi-stage journey designed to translate imaginative concepts into high-end gallery works. Every finished piece follows a structured development cycle:

  • Concept Development & Research: Every project begins with extensive research into anatomy, lore, and classical reference to build a solid narrative foundation.
  • Traditional Illustration & Digital Modification: Initial sketches are refined through traditional illustration, then moved into Procreate or Photoshop. Here, I modify the composition and create precise line art or digital mock-ups to finalize lighting and form.
  • Color Studies: Before committing to the final surface, I create simplified versions of the piece in watercolor or acrylics. These studies allow me to solve color and value challenges rapidly.
  • Final Execution: The process culminates in the final masterpiece, executed exclusively in Oil. This allows for the depth, texture, and permanence required for my professional portfolio.


Fine Art & Studio

The Discovery Phase: Fantasy & Classical Interests

My primary focus is currently exploring the intersection of Classical technique and Fantasy themes. I am in a dedicated discovery phase, using this time to refine my technical skills and identify the specific subject matter that will form my long-term professional portfolio. My goal is to develop a body of work that merges traditional academic principles with imaginative storytelling.

The Antique Booth: Studies & Revenue

To support my practice and fund the cost of professional materials, I maintain an active booth at a local Antique Store. This outlet is essential for my studio's revenue and serves as a place for my technical studies and experimental pieces. When I explore a new technique, practice an academic style outside of my core interest, or paint on vintage decor and tiles, those works are sold through the antique market. This allows me to test various methods while generating the resources necessary to sustain my fine art development.

Teaching & Artist Resources

I am committed to helping others navigate the realities of being a working artist. I offer one-on-one private tutoring through Wyzant, and I maintain an extensive resource library on Patreon.

On Patreon, I share my entire professional process—not just the finished art, but the "business of art" and how I support my practice through various revenue streams. I provide a variety of practical resources for artists of all levels, including:

  • Downloadable outlines and instructions for study.
  • Video lessons with voiceovers detailing specific techniques.
  • Behind-the-scenes insights into managing a multi-faceted art career.
